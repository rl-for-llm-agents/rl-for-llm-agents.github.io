Workshop on Reinforcement Learning for LLM-based Agents (RL4LLM-Agents)

Co-located with ICAIF 2026

About The Workshop

Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly being deployed as autonomous agents capable of reasoning, planning, and taking actions in complex environments. Reinforcement Learning (RL) offers a powerful framework to train and fine-tune such agents — enabling them to optimize long-horizon objectives, explore effectively, and adapt to dynamic, feedback-rich environments.

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners at the intersection of RL and LLM-based agents, with a particular focus on applications in finance and economics — a domain rich with sequential decision-making problems, non-stationary dynamics, and high-stakes outcomes.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions on the use of Reinforcement Learning techniques for training, fine-tuning, and deploying LLM-based agents. We especially welcome works that address applications in finance, trading, risk management, and related domains. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • RL-based fine-tuning of LLMs — RLHF, RLAIF, GRPO, PPO, and other RL-from-feedback methods for shaping agent behavior.
  • LLM agents for sequential decision-making — planning, reasoning, and acting in multi-step environments.
  • Reward modeling and shaping for LLM agents, including sparse reward settings and reward hacking mitigation.
  • Multi-agent LLM systems — cooperation, competition, and emergent behavior among multiple LLM-based agents.
  • Exploration and sample efficiency in training LLM agents with RL.
  • LLM agents in financial applications — algorithmic trading, portfolio management, market simulation, risk assessment.
  • Agentic RAG and tool use — LLM agents that retrieve information, use APIs, and interact with external environments.
  • Evaluation benchmarks and datasets for RL-trained LLM agents.
  • Safety, alignment, and robustness of RL-trained LLM agents in high-stakes domains.
  • Theoretical foundations — convergence, generalization, and optimization in RL for language models.

Submission

We welcome the following types of submissions:

  • Full papers (up to 8 pages, excluding references) — presenting mature research with rigorous evaluation.
  • Short papers / extended abstracts (up to 4 pages, excluding references) — presenting early-stage work, position papers, or proofs-of-concept.

Submissions should be formatted using the ACM proceedings template and submitted as PDF. All submissions will be peer-reviewed. Accepted papers will be presented at the workshop (oral or poster, based on reviewer recommendations).

Submission portal: To be announced.

Submissions must describe original, unpublished work and must not be under review elsewhere. We follow a single-blind review process.
